Research Associate

I'm a research assistant at Southern University of Science and Technology. In 2010, I received my master's degree in signal processing from the School of Electronic Information of Northwestern Polytechnical University. In 2015, I received my doctorate degree in speech signal processing from Delft University of Technology. From 2015 to 2018, I worked as a researcher in machine learning and image restoration in Delft University of Technology. Since 2018, he has been engaged in the research work of machine learning and signal detection and repair in Southern University of Science and Technology. and is committed to solving practical problems by combining traditional signal processing methods and machine learning methods.

Publications

Aiming at the problem of speech enhancement in wireless voice sensor networks with mesh topology structure, the accuracy, robustness, timeliness and time consistency of the algorithm are studied from four methods, and nearly 10 papers are published in top conferences and journals. Aiming at the problem of fading and aging of van Gogh's works, modeling and learning of fading and aging of ancient paintings were conducted by using machine learning method based on actual data. Relevant research results were published in international conferences and journals related to cultural heritage protection, adopted by Van Gogh Museum and reported by Dutch media.
